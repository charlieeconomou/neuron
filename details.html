Details

A quick look at what Neuron is, why it matters for programming education, and how we’re building it around real development workflows.

01

Overview

Neuron is a classroom-aligned AI workflow for programming education. It follows instructor-defined rules and keeps students focused on understanding—not shortcuts.

For instructors

Set AI rules by assignment

Define what help is allowed, when it’s allowed, and what “good use” looks like.

For students

Guided help, not answer dumps

Support that teaches debugging and reasoning instead of replacing the work.

For courses

Clarity when questions come up

Provide context when needed—without “gotcha” monitoring or detection tools.

02

Why this matters

AI is already part of modern software development. Education needs a learning-first way to use it responsibly—consistent, transparent, and aligned with course goals.

  • Students get mixed messages about what’s allowed, leading to inconsistent use and confusion.
  • Large classes limit feedback—students often need help outside office hours.
  • Departments want visibility into AI use without policing students or relying on detection.
03

Built around real dev tools

We’re developing VS Code extensions and a terminal interface so Neuron can understand full project context—not just single prompts.

Full context

Designed for real workflows

Neuron fits how students actually code: in editors, terminals, and projects—not isolated chat windows.

Learning & control

No agentic editing (for now)

Students stay hands-on. Instructors can allow or restrict direct debugging behaviors by policy.
